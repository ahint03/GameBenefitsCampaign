Are Video Games Actually a Waste of Time?

Looking at how games can support learning, stress management, and development

The Concern

Video games are often treated as distractions, especially when it comes to younger players. It’s common to worry about things like screen time, school performance, or whether games are replacing more “productive” activities. Because of this, gaming is often seen as something that needs to be limited rather than something that could have value.

A Different Way to Look at It

While those concerns aren’t completely unfounded, they don’t tell the full story. Instead of only asking whether video games are harmful, it might be more useful to ask what players are actually getting out of them. In many cases, games are doing more than just filling time.

What Research Shows

Video games can support problem-solving, motivation, and even emotional regulation when used in moderation.

Many games require players to think critically, adapt to new situations, and learn through trial and error. This kind of structure mirrors how real-world skills are developed. Research has shown that games can increase feelings of competence and motivation, which are both important for learning and overall well-being.

In addition to learning benefits, games can also help manage stress. Because they require active participation, players often become fully immersed, which allows them to step away from outside pressures and reset mentally. This kind of focused engagement has been linked to improved mood and reduced stress levels.

Why This Matters

Looking at video games only as distractions ignores the ways they can support development. When used intentionally, games can build problem-solving skills, encourage persistence, and even provide a sense of accomplishment that carries over into other areas.

This doesn’t mean that all gaming is automatically beneficial or that limits aren’t important. Instead, it suggests that the conversation should shift from simply restricting games to understanding how they can be used more effectively.

Rethinking the Role of Games

Video games are becoming a bigger part of everyday life, especially for younger generations. Because of that, it’s important to recognize their potential instead of dismissing them outright. When approached thoughtfully, games can support both emotional well-being and learning in ways that traditional methods don’t always reach.

The goal isn’t to argue that games should replace other activities, but to recognize that they can be more than just entertainment.
